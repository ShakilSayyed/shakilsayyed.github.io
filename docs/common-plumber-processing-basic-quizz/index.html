                          Common Plumbing Processing basic question and answers§

                          Level Basic

                          Q-1 What plastic pipes are used in for sanitation, drainage and rain water ?§

                          Mainly PVC **Polyvinyl chloride**and ABS **Acrylonitrile butadiene styrene** are used for the above purposes. PVC comes in different types.
                          1. CPVC: Chlorinated Polyvinyl chloride: They are produced by chlorination of PVC resin to make it more flexible than mere PVC, they can be used for both cold and hot water due to tolerating heat up to 180 degree Fahrenheit.
                          2. PVCu: un-plasticised Polyvinyl chloride:used for many purposes.
                          3. PVCmu or MuPVC BS 5255:Modified un-plasticised Polyvinyl chloride: used for solvent weld waste and overflow pipes.
                          4. CuPVCChlorinated plasticised Polyvinyl chloride: used for solvent weld cold water service pipe in the late 1970s. They have been replaced by new MDPE.
                          • ABS: can easily be recognized by its black color and rigidity and used for drainage lines, waste pipes.
                          • Polypropylene: Widely used in plumbing industry used for waste pipes, cistern, WC siphons and overflow pipe work. It can not be solvent welded.

                          Q-2 What plastic pipes are used for cold and hot water supply and for central heating services ?§

                          1.PEX or PE:Cross linked polyethylene is a good for both hot and cold temperatures.

                          MDPE and HDPE are two kinds of PE that are used in water pipes. MDPE has superseded HDPE ( used in 1980s). They are hard wearing plastics for water and gas pipe fittings.
                          1. PB or PB-1: Polybutylene: It has high temperature and pressure resistance, low noise transmission, low thermal expansion and low thermal transmission.
                          It does not suffer from corrosion or scaling and conforms to BS EN 1057. It is used in wet central heating system and underfloor heating.

                          Q-3 What is PVC ?§

                          It is a lightweight plastic commonly used in many consumer products. Though it is light weight yet robust to be used to withstand heat and pressure. It replaces many old materials used in guttering pipes.

                          Q-4 Why would you use PVCu or UPVC instead of Simple PVC ?§

                          It is called un-plasticised PVC because the plastic used is not softened during manufacturing. Commonly used in windows, doors and guttering systems etc. It is used in place of PVC because of its strengths and has been around since 1980s replacing wood and other materials.

                          Q-5 Is there any difference between PVCU and CPVC ?§

                          No, they are same terms, in UK the term uPVC was common but to meet the European standards the term PVCU was standardised.

                          Q-6 Metal pipes used in cold and hot water supply and central heating§

                          1. Copper tube BS En 1057: They can last for fifty years as it ages it thins out leading to pinhole leaks. R220 and R250 are two commonly used copper pipes in cold and hot water pipes.
                          Copper tubes can be used for cold and hot water, central heating services, gas installation ( oxygen, methane and other gases ), oil system and sanitation systems.

                          1. Chromium coated copper tube: they are used where aesthetics consideration to be made. And plastic coated copper known as kutelex is available where protection from the elements is essential.

                          2. Galvanized Steel or Iron: Galvanized pipe is steel pipe that has been treated with zinc coating. This keeps the water away from corroding the pipe.

                          They were used in pre 60's building or houses, they have shortest span of life up to 40 years and eventually do get corroded.

                          Q-7 Identify the given pipes§

                          Leads pipes are no longer permitted to be used.

                          Q-8 What are different methods of jointing the metal and plastic pressure pipes ?§

                          There are four main methods of jointing the pipes
                          1. Compression: Used in copper and plastics, compression fittings which are made from plastic are known as Philmac fittings.
                          2. Soldered : Only used in copper.
                          3. Push fits : used in plastics.
                          4. Press fits : Used in copper.

                          Fusion Welded: Large underground water mains use fusion welded fittings. Fittings and the pipe are welded together by heat created by electricity.

                          Q-9 What is flaring or belling ?§

                          It is a process of shaping the pipes end by means of using tools.

                          Q-10 How many types of compression joints are used ?§

                          There are two types:
                          • Type A or Non- manipulative: do not require flaring and commonly used in water fittings.
                          • Type B or Manipulative: mostly used for gas lines can also be found in water fittings.

                          Type A consist of compression nut, copper or brass O ring and a fitting body.

                          Q-11 Where is soldered Capillary Joints used ?§

                          Commonly domestic plumbing used soft soldered joints. They are made up of two type of fittings:
                          1. Integral solder ring: It has a bend of lead-free solder housed inside a raised ring on the fitting socket so extra solder is not needed and it gets melted when heated.
                          2. End feed: It does not have solder in the fittings and does not contain a bend.

                          Solders that contain lead are not allowed on pipework used for either hot or cold water supplies

                          Q-12 What are push fits joints ?§

                          They are made either from plastic or metal, as the name suggest joints are made by pushing the pipes to the fitting and do not require soldering or tightening the screws. Commonly used push fits are shown below.

                          They are also known as smart sleeves or pipe support sleeves.

                          It consist of a main body, screw cap, spacer washer, an EPDM O ring and a metal grab with a metal end ring.

                          Metal grab ring and a metal end ring are fitted inside the body.

                          Q-16 What must be considered when using push fits ?§

                          Use inserts before a pipe is pushed into the body The pipe must be properly inserted home into fittings otherwise it will leak.

                          Do use marks if the pipe has marks printed on it make sure a pipe is at least inserted an inch inside the body. Use plastic pipe cutter instead of any other tool ensuring a clean cut to the pipe.

                          Q-17 What is achieved using inserts into the pipe ?§

                          An extra layer of protection is achieved against any damage.
                          • It also ensures that the pipe retains its circular cross section.
                          • It maintains the rigidity of the pipe within the fittings.
                          • The end also let you feel that it is fully inserted and barbs on the sleeve properly locks it in the pipe.

                          Q-18 What are the benefits of using Polybutylene ?§

                          • It does not affect the taste or colour of the water.
                          • There is minimal internal resistance, thereby increasing flow rates.
                          • It is very flexible, even at very low temperatures.
                          • It is non-corrosive.
                          • It involves safe installation processes; no flame is needed or chemicals such as flux required during installation, and it therefore presents no risk to installers.
                          • It has high resistance to frost damage.
                          • It is not affected by water hardness or softness.
                          • It is not affected by chemical central heating inhibitors or anti-freezes.
                          • It is unaffected by micro-biological growth.
                          • It has high impact strength.
                          • It is suitable for heating and cooling applications.
                          • There are a multitude of coil lengths for economical installation with minimal waste.
                          • It has a low environmental impact in terms of soil, water and air pollution.
