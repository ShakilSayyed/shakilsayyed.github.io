                      Drainage and Sanitation

                      June 4, 2023

                      Level Basic

                      Q-1 What is Sanitation and Drainage ?ยง

                      Sanitation is the process where waste is either disposed hygienically or recycled while drainage can be natural or artificial process of removing water from a given area.
                      When dealing with property sanitation and drainage, there exit different types of systems which ensures that strict policy and practices are followed for health and hygienic measures.

                      Q-2 What are Above Ground Discharge Systems ?ยง

                      AGDS or Above Ground Discharge Systems includes sanitary appliances, sanitary pipework, fittings and Drainage Systems. They are also knows as AGSS or Above Ground Sanitation Systems
                      Gravity Rainwater systems also comes in above ground discharge system. In plumbing AGDS / AGSS exist to keep the environment clean and hygienic

                      The waste product is discharged through a trap, down a discharge branch to a soil stack finally to underground Sewer.

                      Q-3 What is studied in AGDS?ยง

                      It covers the following topics
                      1. Traps
                      2. Sanitary Systems
                      3. Pipework Installation, access and materials
                      4. Soundness Testing
                      5. Rainwater and Guttering
                      6. Maintenance and Decommissioning

                      Q-4 What are sanitary appliances ?ยง

                      They can be divided into two groups. One is used for washing purpose and the other is used for human waste removals.
                      For washing purpose the greywater is passed through the waste pipe while blaclwater that comes out from human waste a soil pipe is used.

                      Q-5 What are different AGDS / AGSS systems ?ยง

                      • There exist four different types of systems
                      1. Primary Ventilated Stack System
                      2. Ventilated branch discharge system
                      3. Secondary ventilated stack system
                      4. Stub Stack
                      All of the above system uses one main vertical pipe which consist of different smaller section of pipes hence known is a main Stack Pipe. It consist of two different vertical sections while it is joined diagonally or horizontally by other sections.

                      Q-6 What are different sections of Drainage systemsยง

                      • They are made up of three different sections
                      1. Soil Pipe: The section that takes the human waste through the pipe into the main gutter which is situated into the soil is named a soil pipe . This is the wet part of the stack also known as soil stack pipe
                      2. Vent Pipe: The upper part of the pipe that is used to allow odours from the waste water and solids to be released into the atmosphere is named vent pipe. This is top dry part of the vent stack
                      3. Waste Pipe: These are the pipes which take the waste water away from baths, showers, washbasins, bidets and kitchen sinks. They vary in size depending on the appliance that they are serving. They are usually connected directly to the soil stack but can sometimes be discharged into an external gully.

                      Q-7 Where would you find the recommendations for design, testing, installation and Maintenance for Above Ground non pressure pipework systems ?ยง

                      Approved Document H of 2013 of the Building Regulation
                      Sanitary pipework system should be designed according to the Building Regulations Approved Document G and H and should conform with BS EN 12056:2:2000. It deals with Gravity drainage systems inside buildings.

                      BS 8000:pt 13 covers good workmanship information

                      Q-8 What is the difference between Soil Pipe and a Waste Pipe ?ยง

                      Soil Pipe is designed to transport the soiled water ( black water) from your toilet, urinal or bidet While a Wast Pipe is designed to transport the wastewater ( greywater ) from your sink,shower, bath and washing machine and dishwasher.

                      Q-9 Why Soil and Waste Pipe are different ?ยง

                      The reason to keep them different is to do with the bodily waste that produce methane and other unpleasant and dangerous gasses that must be vented out of the system before reaching the sewer. For this reason soil pipes are designed to be vented through the roof to escape out into the air. The waste pipe does not produce these gases and is not required to be ventilated. Soils pipe also need to be larger in diameter than waste pipes due to handling the solid materials.

                      Making sure the right waste is going down the right drain is important for the health of your household drainage system resulting in healthy and hygienic odour less home environment

                      Q-10 What does Building Regulation Document H contains ?ยง

                      It deals with Drainage and Wast disposal. It has six different Parts or sections named H1 to H6.
                      • Part H1 - Foul water drainage : Contains guidance on sanitary pipework, foul drainage, repair, alteration, sewers etc.
                      • Part H2 - Wastewater treatment system and cesspools: Contains guidance on different types of system and maintenance.
                      • Part H3 - Rainwater drainage: Contains guidance on gutters and rainwater pipes, drainage of paved areas etc.
                      • Part H4 - Building over existing sewers : Contains guidance on undue risk in the event of failure during construction and from settlement.
                      • Part H5 - Separate systems of drainage :
                      • Part H6 : Solid wast storage
                      It was first published in 2010, the amendments happened in 2013 than in 2015. The current amendment is done in 2022.

                      Q-11 Where is the vent in a soil pipe placed ?ยง

                      Building regulations state that the opening of the ventilating pipes should be located at least 900mm above any window in a building that is within three meters of the pipe. .

                      Q-12 What are the rules of terminating the Vent Pipeยง

                      The vent pipe termination also be protected with a wire cage or cover to prevent debris or birds entering them. The wire cage could be terminal mesh guard or a Vent Cowl where stack is sited in exposed windy conditions

                      When a vent pipe comes out through the roof ( if Stack pipe is installed inside the building) it will need to be correctly weathered using lead or weathering slate. It is to make sure that rain water does not come into the property.

                      Q-13 How does a soil vent pipe work ?ยง

                      It takes the waste water from upper floor toilets, bath and sinks into the underground drainage system. The pipe is vented to allow odours from the waste to be released into the atmosphere. The vent also ensures the drainage system remains at atmospheric pressure.
                      If the soil pipe is not vented, vacuum would build up behind the waste water as it travels down the pipe resulting the odours and gases from the foul drain to travel up the pipe and enter the building.

                      Q-14 How Soil stack is arranged from bottom up ?ยง

                      • At the base of a soil stack there exist a long radius bend. This allows the effluent to maintain its velocity when chaanging from a vertical to horizantal movement. Sometimes two 45 degree bends can be used as an alternative to provide better movement. The minimum radius bend is fo 20mm.

                      Tight bends can case a problem called compression, where the water hitting the bend forces a shock wave of air upwards, which can blow the water out of waste pipe traps, causing them to lose their seal and left obnoxious smells into the dwelling.

                      Q-15 What is Rodding access point to a Soil Stackยง

                      It is point that can be used to clear the blockage and mostly it is the point where bend occurs. So it is best practice to position a Rodding Access just above the ground level in case of any blockage.

                      Approved Document H, paragraph 1.34, recommends that rodding points should be provided in discharge stacks to give access to any lengths of pipes which cannot be reached from any other part of the system. These access points should be positioned above the spillover level of appliances. BS EN 12056: 2000 also says that the use of of apparatus or equipment should not be impeded by the structure of the other services.

                      Access rodding and clearance should always be allowed for in each discharge branch and also in the main soil stack.

                      Q-16 What are the height restriction for the lowest connection to the soil stack pipe ?ยง

                      It is 450mm minimum for the low-rise up to 3 storeys for single dwelling. For multi storeys building it is 750mm minimum.

                      For more than five storeys building ground floor appliances must discharge into gully drain or its own stack. Similarly if it is more than 20 storeys, ground floor and first floor appliances must discharge into gully, drain or own stack.

                      Q-17 What is Primary Ventilated Stack System ?ยง

                      It consist of a single stack and costs less in terms of materials and installation time, as it does not need separate ventilating pipe like other one-pipe systems. It is regarded good for the business and the environment.
                      .

                      It relies on the discharge branches from the appliances being closely grouped around the stack and therefore does not require extra ventilation. The Soil Stack is large enough to allow pressure fluctuations without affecting the trap seal loss in the system.

                      Soil Stack are designed with either an external balloon grating to allow pressure fluctuations in and out or an air admittance valve to allow air in to balance air pressure. AAVs prevent siphonage in water traps.

                      Q-18 What is waste branch pipework ?ยง

                      Branch discharge pipework is the horizontal pipework connecting bath, showers, hand basins, urinals and kitchen sinks to the vertical stack known as Soil Stack of 110mm or to a 50mm pipeline that runs directly down into the drain.
                      The Branch should always discharge either into another branch pipe, the main discharge stack or a gully.

                      Q-19 Different Parts of waste pipe branchesยง

                      1. Waste Pipe available in different length for different diameter of pipes.
                      2. Tees used to connect two wast pipe ( single Tee) or three waste pipes ( Cross Tee) togehter at 92.5 degree angle.
                      3. Bends used to create a change in direction along waste pipe run.
                      4. Caps used to cap off unused outlets.
                      5. Waste Traps used under sinks, bathtubs, showers and other wastewater appliances to create a water trap that prevents bad smells from coming back up from the drains.
                      6. Air Admittance Valve required in certain long run systems to balance the pressure in the system.

                      There exist more parts not shown in above diagram

                      1. Couplings used for connecting lengths of pipe together
                      2. Pipe Clips used to secure the waste pipes in place and provide support around joints
                      3. Conversion Bends used to create changes in direction while also converting the connection type between push-fit and solvent weld
                      4. Reducers used to convert the diameter of the system between 32, 40 and 50mm as required.

                      Q-20 What is swept entrance branch connection Iยง

                      A large radius bend is called a swept entrance. Branch Connection to the soil stack should be made with a swept entrance or at an angle, to aid the flow of effluent.

                      Suction ( -ve pressure ) is produced in the discharge stack; its magnitude is affected by the radius or slope of the branch inlet. A larger radius ( swept ) or 45 degree entry will minimize the amount of suction while square connection will have the opposite effect.

                      Q-21 What is meant by Self-Cleaning Velocity in Primary Ventilated Stack ?ยง

                      All discharge branches need a continuous fall away from the appliances towards the stack. This gradient should be a maximum of 5 degree but ideally 2.5 degrees which will allow the water to maintain a Self-Cleaning Velocity.
                      The Self-Cleaning Velocity means that the liquid carries the solids as it flows reducing the risk of blockage.

                      Q-23 How to organise branches in soil stack ?ยง

                      The location of the branch pipe in a stack should not cause cross flow into another branch pipe and can be prevented by adhering to given details above.

                      Cross Flow occurs when two branches are located opposite to each other and waste from each branch meets another branch directly rather than down the soil stack. The cross flow could cause loss of trap seal by effluent back-flowing up the opposite connection.

                      The most common sizes of waste pipes in UK are 32mm, 40mm and 50mm. For commercial applications 50mm pipe is used. If a branch connecting to soil pipe has up to 75mm of diameter pipe. It should have 45 degree angle or maximum 25 degree radius.                       Q-26 What if these requirements can not be met due to some reasons ?ยง

                      In this case an extra ventilation to the system will need to be added to safeguard the trap seal. It can be done in two ways:
                      1. By installing a ventilated branch discharge system, where each waste pipe branch is separately ventilated.
                      2. By installing a secondary ventilated stack system, where the waste stack is directly ventilated.

                      Q-27 The Ventilated branch discharge systemยง

                      • This system is used on larger systems or high buildings where there is a risk of trap seal loss because the wast pipe lengths are excessive.

                      Q-28 Rules for Branch ventilated pipesยง

                      Any branch ventilating must be connected to the discharge stack above the spillover level of the appliance.
                      The size of the branch ventilating pipe for a single appliance is a minimum of 25mm, for multiple appliances it is 32mm

                      Control of the pressure in the waste pipe is achieved by ventilating it no further than 750mm from the appliance as this safeguards against trap seal loss by induced or self-siphonage. Alternatively AAVs may be used at each appliance allowing air into the system when the appliance is in operation.

                      Q-29 Secondary Ventilated Stackยง

                      • In this system, the main stack has as secondary vent which is connected from just above the long radius bend going up to the dry section of the stack.

                      The secondary vent allows more air to flow and safeguard against any positive or negative pressure fluctuations.

                      Q-30 How branch connections are made to a soil stack ?ยง

                      • If you are making a soil stack make sure that you buy the parts that comes with proper hole but if you do need to make additional branch connection you can use a hole saw or Hole cutter that fits into your drill machine.

                      • you can use the following to add new connections :

                      1. Strap boss
                      2. Boss branch
                      3. Collared boss
                      4. Waste manifold

                      Q-31 What is a strap boss and how to connect it to the Soil Pipe ?ยง

                      Strap boss allows a waste pope to connect directly to the soil stack. A hole is cut and the boss and strap is glued into the correct position. It can be used to remove fluid from a kitchen sink, basin or from bath.

                      To maintain 2 and half degree gradient, the strap bosses have a top side marked. The correct size boss adaptor is then inserted to create the watertight seal.

                      Q-32 How to use Boss branches ?ยง

                      They allow waste pipe connection to be made without making any additional holes.

                      Q-33 How to use collared bosses ?ยง

                      They are a modification to the standard single-stack system. It was developed by the Marley company for use with the main stack and increase the diameter of the main stack to allow the connection to be made. The collar is a gallery with purpose made bosses for connection of waste pipe to the main soil stack without causing any problem of cross flow.

                      Q-34 How to use a waste manifold ?ยง

                      They are similar to the collared boss and offers multiple connections in restricted area. These are commonly used in bathrooms where the soil stack is internal. They come with four or six connections. They also come in square or arch shape.

                      In plumbing industry it is also known as elephants foot manifold. The above design fits the corner of a room but may come in different design to suit your need. This also fits into your main Soil stack.

                      Q-35 What precaution should be taken if main soil stack is fitted inside the building ?ยง

                      If a soil stack is installed inside the building such as flats or maisonettes, there must be an intumescent collar installed between each living area. This is a fire break that restricts flames and smoke from spreading from one area to another.

                      It is either fitted or rebated and cools down metal, steel and copper pipes not allowing fire to spread into another area.

                      If a soil stack is fitted inside the building and vent pipe can not come out through the roof or needs to be left inside the building, then according to Building Regulations the acceptable way to terminate a soil stack is by installing an air admittance valve ( AAV ). This allows air in if the pressure fluctuates, but it does not allow foul air out.

                      The main purpose of AAV is to allow air into the stack but not to allow the foul air into the building. If it is fitted inside the boxing. There must be access available for maintenance and sufficient ventilation in the boxing for the AAV should be maintained.

                      When installing AAV, manufacturer's instruction must be followed, it must be installed at least 1m above the highest spillover level.

                      Q-36 What are the main System Compliance in AGDS ?ยง

                      1. The depth of water seal in a trap should not be less than a 50mm.
                      2. The waste pipe diameter and gradient must maintain a trap seal depth of at least 25mm after an appliance has been used.
                      3. The nominal diameter of the discharge pipe shall not be reduced in the direction of flow.
                      4. Where air admittance valves are used to vent drainage systems, they shall be adequately sized and manufacturer’s instructions must be followed.
                      5. Access should also be provided above the spillover level of the highest appliance.

                      Q-37 What is Stub Stack System ?ยง

                      A stub stack is a cut-off version of an SVP ( Soil Vent Pipe), which is fitted with an air admittance valve to prevent foul smells

                      The maximum distance a stub stack can be installed away from the main vented stack is of 6m.

                      Gullies and Trapsยง

                      Q-1 What is a gulley in drainage ?ยง

                      A gully in drainage system is a drainage fitting with an open top sitting on concrete base and an outlet to one or more sides.

                      Gullies can be used to connect wastewater outlet to suitable drains. Gullies fall into two main categories. Road gullies and domestic gullies.

                      Q-2 How domestic drainage gullies are designed ?ยง

                      They are installed outside and needed wherever there is a water discharge point around the outside of your property is present.
                      • The term gully trap often used to describe the gulley itself. It refers to an aspect fo the internal design of the gully. The design can change between different type of gullies but the general purpose is the same.
                      • When water flows down through the center of the gully into a basin and back up towards an outlet pipe connected to the drain, a small amount will remain at the bottom between the outlet and the central chamber acting like a trapped seal to prevent any small escaping back out of the drain into the air.

                      Q-3 What are Tubular traps ?ยง

                      They are normal traps but named Tubular as they are long, round and hollow like a tube. They are also called Swivel traps because a coupling between two parts enabled only one to revolve without turning the other.

                      • They come in either P or S or running styles. The union connection in the centre allows the trap to swivel through 360 degree, enabling multiple positions and connections.

                      Q-4 What is the reasons traps are used ?ยง

                      The main reason they are attached to the underside of the wast fittings on the appliance is to stop unpleasant smells entering from the soil stack into the property.

                      • They do this by using a plug of water as a seal.

                      • Traps comes in different styles to suit various situations. They are commonly made of Polypropylene while other are made of brass and copper. Most of them are push fits using compression method with a rubber seal.

                      • All traps has to maintain a water level known as trap seal. The depth of the seal depands upon the diameter of the trap and for what appliance it is being used for.

                      Almost all appliances connected to a soil stack should maintain a depth of 75mm with some exception to this rule.

                      1. WCs’ can have 50mm seal, it is due to the reason that WCs’ discharge pipe is large in diameter having very slim chance of siphonage. Similarly bath and shower can also have the depth of 50mm due to them being build in with slight fall that refills the trap due to trickle effect.

                      2. Some appliances are allowed to discharge into an external gully and can have the seal depth reduced to a 38mm. It is due to the reason that there is an air break present between the water level in the gully and the pipe so there is a less chance of smells coming back into the property.

                      The details of trap seal death is given in table 1.0 in Q-22.

                      Q-5 Where are P and S traps used ?ยง

                      A U-shaped P trap is commonly installed when the discharge branch is horizontal to the appliance.

                      • A common issue associated with P-traps is the accumulation of debris in the bend over the time resulting in slow drain. In this case it needs to be thoroughly cleaned out manually. If the appliance is used rarely it can eventually vent sewer gases into a living area, because of evaporation over the course of several weeks. It can easily be remedied by running water through drains periodically.
                      An S-shaped S trap is commonly installed when the initial waste pipe that leads to the discharge branch is vertical.

                      • The problem with S trap is that sometimes too much water flows through an s-trap, leaving the trap nearly empty which results in forming the air gap allowing gases to escape through the trap. This is knowns as dry trap and are not recommended.

                      Q-6 What are running traps ?ยง

                      It is depressed U-shapped section of a pipe in the drain called running trap. It allows the free passage of fluid but always remains full whatever the state of the pipe forming a seal against the passage of gasses.

                      It is commonly found where more than one appliances need to be trapped together as individual traps may not be possible due to many reasons. They may be found in public toilets where one running trap is used for a range of unstrapped washbasins.

                      The running trap is installed into the discharge branch itself.

                      Q-7 What is a bottle trap and where is it used ?ยง

                      The trap being in the shape of bottle is known as a bottle trap. They are easier to install and appears to be very neat in installation.

                      They should be avoided on places s where they can be prone to blockage due to accumulation of food or other deposits. So they are not suited under a kitchen sink.

                      Chrome plated traps are installed where waste pipe is visible so both pipe and traps gives a neat finish.

                      Q-8 What are slim line, pedestal or straight through traps ?ยง

                      They are designed for use under a washbasin hidden behind the pedestal.

                      They are called slim because they are slimmed S trap, designed to save space

                      The problem with these traps is that they slow down the water flow being very slim.

                      Q-9 Which trap is used in washing machine ?ยง

                      An extended P trap is used for not only washing machine but dishwasher due to both appliances being pumped discharge.

                      The discharge house fits over the top of the trap. Make sure the length of the pipe is higher than the drum to prevent syphoning.

                      The minimum height of washing machine pipe is 18 inches or 457mm and maximum to be 30 inches or 760 mm. In most cases the length of 600mm would suffice.

                      Kitchen sink can also be connected to the washing machine by using the spigot trap to avoid additional piping.

                      Q-10 What is Spigot trap ?ยง

                      In plumbing the term spigot is used to provide an insertion point by using a peg or plug into an existing trap.

                      It is often used to connect a washing machine or a dishwaher to an existing P trap used for another purpose.

                      Q-11 What traps are used in Bath ?ยง

                      They are specially designed for the restricted area under a fitted bath and are allowed to maintain 50mm depth of seal.

                      If a bath is discharged into a hopper or gully trap, 38mm trap seal is allowed.

                      38mm seal traps are called low-level traps.

                      Q-12 Shower traps and how to use them ?ยง

                      Only the design of shower traps are different but the principle remains the same, it traps the water in the drain and release it slowly. They include access for cleaning via removable waste grill.

                      It is important to keep it clean and free from blockages to ensure optimal drainage and to prevent any unpleasant smells.

                      Q-13 Anti-Vac Or Resealing traps ?ยง

                      They have small air admittance valve on their body to allow a small amount of air into the waste pipe. It is done to avoid any siphonage under negative pressure.

                      They are not advised to be installed on pipe work that is due to be Air Tested as they may not hold the pressure. Hence not suitable for new installations.

                      They are used in places where siphonage is suspected to occur resulting in trap seal loss and also known as anti-syphon traps. They can be used in places where system is upgraded and additional appliances are added.

                      Though they may look same as other traps but internally they have a bypass tube which allows air to travel from one side of the seal to the other whilst being discharged.

                      Q-14 What is a self-sealing waterless valve ?ยง

                      It can be used as an alternative to a trap. It uses an internal membrane as a seal. It allows water to flow in one direction only and closes to prevent foul air from entering the building.

                      It can be used on the system meeting BS EN 12056 Pt-2.

                      Q-15 What are the advantages of using self-sealing waterless valve / trap ?ยง

                      It is often the case when additional appliances are used on an existing system, the previous requirements do not meet up the BS EN 12056 standard. And one needs to change the existing piping. To avoid this problem a self-sealing waterless valve can be used It provides the following benefits.
                      1. Removing the problem of negative pressure.
                      2. No water traps/seals means no possible siphonage.
                      3. Operation remain silent.
                      4. Installed on the same discharge branch without affecting the performance.
                      5. Can withstand the back pressure.
                      6. Pipework length is no longer restricted.
                      7. Gradient is not restricted too.

                      It was invented by a company called Osma and are known as HepvO valve/trap.

                      Q-16 What forms the seal in traps ?ยง

                      It is a water trapped in a trap that forms a seal. If this water is lost, there is no seal present which results in foul smells entering from the pipes into atmosphere.

                      Q-17 What faults can cause the loss of trap seal ?ยง

                      • There can be number of faults present in pipework for trap seal problems
                      1. Long waste pipes i.e, exceeding the required length.
                      2. Very small waste pipe or too small for the appliance.
                      3. Pipes are installed with incorrect fall.
                      4. Incorrect bends at the foot of the soil stack.
                      5. Too many appliances on the same waste branch.
                      6. Too many changes of direction.

                      Q-18 What are the main reasons for loss of trap seal ?ยง

                      • There are various ways in which tap/trap seal loss occurs ?
                      1. Self-siphonage
                      2. Induced-siphonage
                      3. Compression
                      4. Wavering outlet
                      5. Evaporation
                      6. Capillary attraction
                      7. Momentum
                      8. Foaming

                      Q-19 What is self-siphonage ?ยง

                      It occurs when water is discharged from an appliance leaving the negative pressure behind with a partial vacuum in the waste pipe. This then pull the water from the trap resulting in seal loss.

                      In most cases it avoided by adhering to the required length. But if it does occur, the installation of the vent on the waste pipe branch may be necessary or an anti-siphon trap could be fitted.

                      Q-20 What is induced siphonage ?ยง

                      If more than one appliance is connected to same discharge branch, one appliance can cause the siphonage to occur in the other appliance, this phenomena is known as induced siphonage.

                      When the water is discharged down from the one appliance, the water in the trap of the next appliance is pushed in by a negative pressure causing the loss of trap seal. For this reason multiple appliances should have a discharge branch of 50mm.

                      Q-21 How compression causes trap seal loss ?ยง

                      • All Waste pipe are eventually connected to a Soil stack, if this soil stack base is connected to tight radius bend, the water coming down from the stack can not flow smoothly. The tight bend causes the compression to occur which in result build up the positive pressure and affects the lower appliance trap seal as shown in diagram below.

                      Q-22 What is Wavering out effect and how it affects trap seal loss ?ยง

                      It is caused by high winds. The effect of the wind blowing across the op of the vent pipe causes trap seal water to move like a wave resulting in trap seal loss.

                      It can be prevented by the use of vent cowl.

                      Q-23 How evaporation causes a trap seal loss ?ยง

                      It happens due t lack of use of appliances. If an appliances is not used for a long time, the water in traps evaporates resulting in seal loss. If it happens bad smells starts to enter into the property.

                      Since all traps rely on water seal, they are expected to be used regularly.

                      Q-24 How a momentum causes a trap seal loss ?ยง

                      If a large amount of water is suddenly discharged down the trap, the force of the water will cause the trap seal water to leave its position resulting in seal loss

                      Running a normal tap water rebuild the seal.

                      Q-25 How capillary action causes a trap seal loss ?ยง

                      If a trap gets filled with debris, it narrows down the passage for flow of water resulting in capillary action that pushes the trap seal water out of its designated space.

                      Sometime long hair can cause this to happen.

                      Q-26 What is the common reason for trap seal loss ?ยง

                      The most basic reason for trap seal loss is the leak in traps that happens due to one of the rubber seals not being watertight or the plastic body being damaged resulting is loss of water.

                      Q-27 Drainage gradient and Fallยง

                      All horizontal drainage above and below the grounds including waste pipe should be installed to an adequate gradient. To calculate the fall of any pipe from one point to another, you need to measure its horizontal length knowns as the distance and multiply to a given gradient.

                      • Use the formulae Fall = Gradient X Distance.

                      The correct gradient gives adequate flow velocity which is needed for the correct operation of removing the waste without having any blockage.

                      It is estimated that the gradient from 1 in 40 to 1 in 110 will give correct flow velocity. This is the recommended ideal fall for a waste pipe.

                      Note: 1 in 40 or 1 in 110 is not the fall measurement but the ratio of the gradient.

                      If the gradient is not steep enough i.e, less than 1 in 110, then the pipe could block the solid and it becomes stranded.

                      Q-28 Calculate the gradient and decides if it is correct for the flow velocity ?ยง

                      1. If a 1m section of 32mm waste pipe has a fall of .013m what is the gradient.
                      Fall = Gradient X Distance OR Gradient = Fall / Distance

                      First change the fall from meter into millimeter.

                      • Gradient = Fall / Distance => .013 X 1000 / 1 = 13
                      The answer 13 falls into 1 in 40 thus assume to be correct gradient used.

                      Q-29 Calculate the fall for given valuesยง

                      • For a given pipe length of 3m use in a waste pipe with the gradient of 1 in 80.

                      • We know that Fall = Gradient X Distance.

                      First change the gradient from given ration ( 1 / 80 = 0.0125 )
                      • Fall = .0125 X 3 = 0.0375m = 37.5 mm.

                      • A 50m section of foul water pipe work is used in fitting while the gradient is to be 1 in 80.

                      • Fall = ( 1/80 ) X 50 = 0.625m or 625mm

                      Soundness testing and commissioning AGSยง

                      Q-1 In how many ways drain test can be performed ?ยง

                      • There are two main methods for a drain test Water test and Air test.
                      • Other methods include smoke testing and mandrel testing.
                      The above test are carried out to make sure newly installed plumbing system is free of leaks and is up to standard.

                      The correct procedure of drain testing is vital to ensure a completed project which should falls in line with any standing regulations in the UK.

                      Usually test is performed before official inspection. The test is then again performed in the presence of a building control officer. It is must that Building Control officer to sign off your drain test.

                      Q-2 Which drainage test is to be carried out in domestic property ?ยง

                      An Air Test is usually carried out to all pipe work following installation, while a Water Test is only required to verify a failed air test or to test manholes or inspection chambers ( IC )
                      A failure during an Air Test is not enough to scrap the pipeline, If an Air Test indicates a leak in the system, a water test must be then undertaken to verify and quantify the problem.

                      Q-3 What needs to be looked at when testing the AGDSยง

                      Make sure there is no visible leak and the performance of the system as defined Per BS EN 12056.
                      If the system is large or installed in a multi storey building. The system will need to be tested in stages.

                      Q-4 How a visual inspection is done when doing Soundness testing ?ยง

                      3. Correct gradients at correct angle is used.

                      Q-10 What is the commissioning process for AGDS ?ยง

                      1. As with most other systems, sanitary pipework system must be in operation as a part fo the commissioning process.
                      2. Give it a full visual inspection, no loose connection and all traps must be full before testing.
                      3. Once your test kit parts are correctly fitted into pipe work, start testing the pipework for Soundness as described in BS EN 12056
                      4. Finally carry out a performance test.

                      Q-11 How to do Air Test for AGPS ?ยง

                      1. Using the hand bellow increase the air pressure in the Pipe system up to38mm or 3.8 mile bar then turn off the inlet valve off so no air can escape through the pump.
                      2. Leave the system pressurised for 3 minutes. If there is no pressure drop noticed in manometer, your Air Test for soundness of Above Ground Drainage system is successful.
                      3. If the pressure is dropped, find a leak in the system using the leak spray or soapy water to see the bubble coming out from the joints. Repair the system and repeat the test.

                      Note: if it is a cast iron stack you can use smoke pellets to detect the leak, but it can not be used with plastic pipe as it can degrade the plastic seals used in Soil stack.

                      Once the soundness test is done and passed, you can carry out the Performance Test.

                      Q-13 What is a performance test ?ยง

                      It is a test to ensure that the all traps present in pipe work system, whether discharged singly or in groups should drain speedily, quietly and completely while retaining water seal during peak working condition.
                      Performance test is carried out under maximum demand. And to achieve maximum demand we would need to fill all the appliances including the bath to their over flow levels.

                      Q-14 How to carry out Performance Test on Pipe work ?ยง

                      1. All the appliances are filled with water to their over flow levels.
                      2. The water is then released along with all WCs are flused at the same time.
                      3. Once done, check that the all trap seals are not less than 25mm using a matt black dipstick.

                      The test should be carried out three times. While testing is carried out you need to make people aware that testing is going on and when it is safe to use appliances.

                      If tests are done periodically, this would be noted down in a maintenance record.

                      Q-16 What is decommissioning in plumbing ?ยง

                      When a plumber removes old sanitary appliances and replace them with new one. It is called decommissioning the above ground drainage systems. It requires careful planning and procedure.
                      Risk assessments must be carried out prior to any work is started.

                      Q-17 What is difference between temporary and permanent decommissioning ?ยง

                      If any portion of Soil stack or waste pipe being replaced it comes under temporary decommissioning. It is essential that your customer is made aware of the work being in progress. During temporary decommissioning a portion of existing pipe work is taken out for a short period of time and replaced with a new one.
                      A permanent decommissioning is carried out when soil stack, waste pipe or appliances are taken out of service while minimizing the risk to people and environment securing the place properly. It can happen if you are renovating your home or business and services are not used for a longer time and may or may not be replaced with new one in future.

                      5. Use barriers and warning sign when in danger.

                      Rainwater systemsยง

                      Q-1 How roofs affect rain water system ?ยง

                      There are three main factors which affects the amount of the rain water collected
                      1. Size of the roof
                      2. Angel of the roof
                      3. Velocity of the rain water

                      Q-2 How rainwater system is installed on domestic building ?ยง

                      Commonly gutters are installed on wooden facia board that collects the rain water which flows through downpipe under the force of gravity and discharged away into some area.

                      Q-3 What is the main purpose of rainwater system ?ยง

                      1. To protect the property’s foundation.
                      2. Restrict ground erosion.
                      3. Prevent water penetration into the property.
                      4. Provide a means of water collection.

                      Q-4 What is to be considered when collecting the rainwater ?ยง

                      It must be collected from any property in such a way that it does not :

                      1. create a nuisance for the occupier
                      2. damage the property or its adjacent property
                      Any rainwater system installed must have adequate capacity to cope with the expected flow of rainwater.

                      Q-5 Where can the rainwater be discharged away ?ยง

                      It can be discharged away into one of the five areas:
                      1. Surface water drainage: These are public sewer
                      2. Combined sewer: Used with combined system of drainage
                      3. Water course: River or stream
                      4. Soakaway drain: Specially designed pit sited within the boundary which allows water to naturally soak away.
                      5. Rainwater harvesting: Rain water is reused within the property

                      Q-6 Who manages Surface Water Drainage ?ยง

                      There are 10 water and sewerage companies in England and Wales. These companies are responsible for removing and processing the rain water which comes from properties and discharged into a separate system of drainage known as Surface Water Drain ( public sewer)

                      Q-7 What is the rainwater intensity ?ยง

                      It is a measure of the amount of rain that falls over time. The rainfalls is measured in litres/second/meter square ( l/s/meter square).

                      It is a big factor and must be considered into rainwater system design. You should know what is the rain intensity in your region where you need to install the system.

                      Q-8 How Rainwater Harvesting is done ?ยง

                      • To harvest rainwater, storage cisterns are installed below ground. They collect rainwater from gullies and store water. Then they are pumped into property cold water cistern, installed in the loft, to be used by toilets present in the property.

                      Just like rainwater harvesting, greywater harvesting is also used to store the water and feed it to the toliet flush.

                      Q-9 What are rules for guttering ?ยง

                      1. When fixing gutters on the property they should be laid to a fall in such a way so that water runs smoothly.
                      2. This fall depends upon the pitch and size of the roof above your guttering.
                      3. Most manufacturer agree to gutter fall of 1:600 ratio. So every 600mm length of the pipe 1mm drop is maintained or 25mm in 15m.
                      4. The gradient fo the roof eaves should not allow the rainwater to run off the roof and miss gutter.
                      5. Straight runs of guttering give maximum flow rate.
                      6. When change in direction happens rainwater outlet should be used.
                      7. The more outlet on the system, the less distance rainwater has to cover results in increase of discharge efficiency.
