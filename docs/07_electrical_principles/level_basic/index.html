                      Electrical Principles§

                      Level Basic

                      Q-1 What is the current relevant UK standards for the electrical work ?§

                      BS 7671:2018
                      It covers requirements for design, erection and verification of electrical installations to provide safety and proper functioning for the intended use.

                      Q-2 Some standard also use EN after BS what does it mean ?§

                      EN indicates European number, a number shows that it meets not only British standard but also European standards
                      -

                      Q-3 Why Building Regulations Documents are important ?§

                      Because it not only covers the design and construction of the building but also cover health and safety of people in the building.
                      Note: It states that in most cases people carrying out work should arrange for a third party to check that works meets the required standards within the Regulation subsections.

                      Q-4 Which Part under Regulations’ subsections covers Electrical porvisions ?§

                      Electrical Work comes under Part P.
                      This document provides guidance on common building Electrical work.

                      Q-5 What Electrical jobs are required to be notified ?§

                      1. Installing a new circuit. 2. Replacing a consumer unit. 3. Adding or altering an existing circuit in a special location.

                      Q-6 Name two different types of Current supplies used in domestic plumbing Systems§

                      1. Alternating current known as AC. 2. Direct current knows as DC.
                      AC current makes a sinusoidal wave wile DC current a straight line

                      Q-7 What is difference between current and voltage ?§

                      A current is said to be a flow of charge either positife or negative from one direction to another. While to push the current to flow the force or potential required is known as voltage.
                      The unit of current is ampere ( amp) while the unit of voltage is volt ( v )

                      Q-8 Which current cycles are measured in Hertz§

                      Since AC current travels uniformly in alternate direction throughout the circuit. Its cycles are calculated in one second, known as hertz.
                      DC current travels in a straight line.

                      Q-9 What Advantages does AC has over DC ?§

                      AC Voltage can be easilty be transformed to higer or lower voltage while DC voltage are not easily transformed.
                      AC can easily be transported over long distances without excessive voltage loss

                      Q-10 What is conventional current ?§

                      In DC circuit, charge or electron flows from one pole to another pole. That is from the negative to positive. But in the beginning scientist assumed that current flows from positive to negative terminal and it is known as conventional current.

                      Q-11 What is a consumer unit ?§

                      A consumer unit formerly called a fuse box is a device that is installed as close as possible to supply company equipment. A company that supplies the Electricity installs its Electric meter from this point the wires enters the consumer unit that controls the house hold eclectic supply. It must be fitted with individual circuits breaker called MCB and RCD which controls house hold circuits in the event of any short circuit occurring.
                      When the electricity leaves from the consumer unit it reaches the home wall and ceiling in two ways either in a series or in parallel. A series ciruit is also known as a radial circuit while a prarallel circuit is also known as Rign circuit.

                      Q-12 What is a Radial Circuit Or Series Circuit ?§

                      It is a circuit that covers overhead lights or wall light in turn. It starts from the consumer unit and connects to every point on the circuit terminating at the last one.
                      The lightening circuit is usually fed by a 1.5 mm twin and earth PVC insulated cable and it is protected by a 6amp MCB in consumer unit. lightening circuits are usually separated by two seprate circuits one if for upstairs and one for downstairs.

                      Q-13 What is a Ring main circuit Or Parallel circuits ?§

                      A continuous ring circuit usually feeds sockets that are kept at the bottom of the wall to provide electricity to T.V, Stereos, computer and other accessories. Unlike Radial circuits they do not end to the point but goes back to the consumer unit.
                      The Ring main circuits allows the cables to be kept to an optimize size as Electricity is permitted to flow in two directions to reach the socket. It is feed using a 2.5mm twin and earthed PVC cable and is protected by a 32amp circuits protection device known as MCB or RCD

                      What are spur outlets ?§

                      They are placed on existing system where a scoket from the ring main is not possible to be placed. It is connected to the ring main through the junction box. They can be either fused or non fused.

                      What are Earthing systems ?§

                      All dwelling should have an earthing system to protect the household from a fatal shock. There are mainly three types of commonly used system.
                      These systems are knowns as 1. TT system 2. TN-S system 3. TN-C-S system

                      Describe a TT system ?§

                      It is found in old houses and in rural areas. In this system all exposed metal work is connected directly to earth via a large copper rod stuck directly into the ground outside.
                      It uses an earth electrode
                      • Picture

                      What is TN-S system ?§

                      In this system the earth conductor wire is connected back to the metallic sheath of the distributor's service cable as it enters the property. This sheathing provides a separate neutral and earth route back to the sub-station supply transformer.

                      Describe the TN-C-S earthing system ?§

                      In this system neutral cable is earthed close to the source of supply and at points throughout the distribution system. There is also a neutral to earth connection at the intake of the installation.
                      The C means the supplier uses a `combined earth and neutral`. while the S means the earth and neutral are separate within the customer's property. It is also called `PME` and uses `PEN` conductor which links `cps & neutral

                      What is a Temporary bonding ?§

                      When working in dwellings all exposed metal work is supposed to be propelry earthed. If your work requires a pipe to be extended you would be cutting the pipe to do the job. Whether a pipe or connection being extended or not, if you cut the metal, it is your responsibility to use a 10 square mm wire sheathed as earth which is attached to a crocodile clips which you need to attach to each end to provide a continuous connectivity throughout the system.
                      If paint is present on the metallic pipes, make sure you remove the paint and to enalbe it to a sound connection with clips.

                      What protection methods are used on electrical systems ?§

                      Different types of protective device are used in consumer unit. They are given as follows: 1. `CB` or Circuit Breaker. 2. `RCD` or Residual current device. 3. `RCBO` or Residual Current breaker with overload device. 4. `Fuses` or Rewirable fuses.

                      How to turn of the home supply of Electricity ?§

                      Consumer unit is fitted with a circuit breaker that controlls the house main electricity. You need to turn it off manually if you want to cut off the house electricity. It is an automatic switch designed to protect an electrical circuit from damage caused by overload or short circuit.

                      How individual circuits are protected in household wiring ?§

                      • An RCD fitted in consumer unit is used to protect the group of circuits. It is fitted with test/reset button on its front.
                      • It constantly monitors the electric circuit and sensitive to pick anything that should not be present in the circuit or at the receiving end.
                      • It can even detect the wrong path of current flowing such as human being and will turn off the electric circuit within a fraction of a second and prevent the person getting electric shock.

                      What is difference between RCD and RCBO ?§

                      • RCBO combines the function of both CB and RCD in one unit. They are used to protect particular circuts istead of having a single RCD for a whole building.

                      what type of cable is used to to connect a boiler to a fused spar ?§

                      Heat resistant cable such as 3 core butyl

                      Where shoud a single core thermoplastic must be used within ?§

                      Conduit or Trunking

                      What is GS38 guidance ?§

                      GS38 document is produced by H&E executive dealing with electrical test equipment
                      It states that test leads must staify certain criteria in order to comply with GS38.

                      Where would you find electrical safe isolation process ?§

                      In GS38 guidance document

                      What wiring is used in domestic properties ?§

                      Twin and Earth ( Flat Profile)

                      What is the size of main earthing conductor ?§

                      `16 square mmm`
                      In Equipotential bonding 10 square mm wire is used

                      What is a Meter tails ?§

                      A piece of wire that connect the electricity meter to the cut out ( also known as service head or supplier fuse) in order to provide power to the dwelling's consumer unit.
                      The size of the meter tail is of `25 square mm`

                      What is Part M of Building regulations ?§

                      Part M building regulations is an approved document which provides information about the ease of access to, and use of, buildings, including facilities for disabled visitors or occupants, and the ability to move through a building easily.
                      The heights of switches and sockets can be found in Part M. While Electrical safety is found in Part L documents

                      What are R1 & R2 values are associated with ?§

                      Resistance of Line & Earth Conductors

                      What does dead polarity show or means ?§

                      It ensures that the circuit is switched through the `Line Conductor`
                      Live polarity ensures that the incoming supply is `not crossed`

                      The Earth or earthing symbol is associated with what class ?§

                      Class I ( that indicates that something needs earthing or earth wiring)

                      What is class II electrical appliance and with what symboel it is indicated ?§

                      A Class II or double insulated electrical appliance uses reinforced protective insulation in addition to basic insulation. Hence, it has been designed in such a way that it does not require a safety connection to electrical earth (ground).

                      What is the other name of Earth wire ?§

                      It is known as `CPC` it stands for `Circuit protective conductor`
                      BS7671 defines a circuit protective conductor (CPC) as a protective conductor connecting exposed-conductive-parts of equipment to the main earthing terminal. United Kingdom an Earth wire is also known as CPC or c.pc.

                      What type of meter used for continunity of c.pc ?§

                      Low Resistance Ohm meter

                      What kind of meter is connected in series and has a very low internal resistance ?§

                      An ammeter
                      In parallel circuit a volt meter is used to measure the potential difference

                      What is clamp meter ?§

                      It is a device that is used to measure current flowing in wire. It allows not to use old method of touching the wire to measure the current.

                      What is a proving unit ?§

                      It is a battery powered portable device used to safely verify the operation of an electrical test tools such as digital multimeter, clamp meters or other electrical testers

                      What is a voltage detector ?§

                      It measures the the voltage in a wire.

                      GS38 documents give more information about it.

                      How to find out the resistance in circuit theoretically ?§

                      • The formula used to measure depends upon the circuit. In series circuit all the resistance are add up to the total resistance. While in parallel circuits resistance are measured using their reciprocals.
                      • So If three resistance of each 10ohms is used. In series circuit the total resistant would be 30 while in parallel circuit it would be 3/10 that is 3.33ohm.
                      Series circuit Rt = R1+R2..., while in parallel circuit the total current is 1/Rt = 1/R1 + 1/R2..

                      Whey a fuse is used in electric appliance ?§

                      Too prevent too much current flow so it should not damage an appliance
                      The fuse used in an eclectic appliance should be rated higher than the operating current
                      • A fuse provides a built-in fail-safe to the electric ciruit for a device.
                      • It contains a thin wire that will metl if the cutrrent gets too high.
                      • It is a safety measure for the device

                      What appliance does not need an Earth wire ?§

                      A double insulated appliance
                      It does not need Earth wire as it is fully encased in plastic with no exposed conductive metal parts such as `hair dryers, mobile phone charger and hand whisks`. These appliance are designed so that there are two levels of insulating material between the electrical parts of the appliance and any parts on the outside that can be touched.

                      A radiator fitted next to the shower would require what type of bonding ?§

                      Supplementary bounding

                      The correct series in which main electricity enters and supply a domestic property ?§

                      Incoming supply --> Suppliers fuse --> Meter --> Consumer Unit --> Appliances circuits

                      What are cables and flex ?§

                      Cables are main wires usually running behind the walls and comes out from the consumer unit to the sockets. While flex runs above floor level and leads for appliances. As the name suggests fles are wires thar are easily turn and twisted that is they are flexible than cables.

                      The size of the cables in domestic properties§

                      • 1.5mm for lightening

                      • 2.5mm for ring main

                      • 6mm for small electric shower up to 7KW

                      • 10mm for large electric shower up to 11KW

                      Different sizes of flex§

                      • 0.5mm used up to 700 w appliances as lights
                      • 0.75mm used up to 1400 W or 1.4KW appliances such as central Heating
                      • 1.0mm used up to 2400 W or 2.4KW appliances such as central heating
                      • 1.5mm used up to 3600 W or 3.6KW appliances such as kettle and immersion heater
                      • 2.5mm used up to 4000 W or 4KW appliances used in commercial
                      • 4.0mm also used in commercial properties.

                      What size of wire and fuse is used for shower and cooker ?§

                      For shower using 6mm cable a `32 amp` RCD is used with 30-32 amp fuse
                      For Cooker using 10mm cable a ` 43 amp` RCD is used with 45 amp fuse.

                      Note: A shower of 11KW also needs `43 amp` MCB or RCD with `45amp` fuse.

                      What are commonly available MCBs ?§

                      They are available in following amperages

                      • 6amp, 10amp, 16amp, 20amp, 32amp, 40amp.

                      What pull cords one would use in a bathroom for a light and a shower ?§

                      A single pole isolator / a single pole pull cord is used for a light while for shower a double pull chord is used.
                      A double pole isolator must be fitted with a manual indicator usually a neon or led light to make it illuminated

                      What are High, Low and Extra voltage ?§

                      • In terms of AC, > 1000 is called High Voltage while 50 to 1000 voltage is regarded as Low Voltage.
                      • < 50 volt is regarded as Extra-low voltage.

                      What is Isolation Procedures for Safe Working on Electrical System and equipment ?§

                      • The rule of safe isolation are:
                      1. Identify the source of supply using an approved voltage indicator.
                      2. Prove that approved voltage indicator or test lamp is functioning correctly.
                      3. Isolate the supply
                      4. Secure the isolation
                      5. Prove the system/equipment is DEAD using an approved voltage indicator or test lamp.
                      6. Put up warning signs to tell other people that the electrical installation has been isolated
                      7. Once the system/equipment is proved DEAD, work can begin.

                      What does a consumer unit should consist of ?§

                      • ON average, each house has six circuits, each controlled by a circuit breaker within your consumer unit.
                      • Commonly there exist two lighting radial circuits one for downstairs and one controlling the upstairs light. They both are controlled by two seprate 6amp MCBs. So if they detect 8amp of continued current, it will trip and stop the flow.
                      • Similarly there would be two separate socket ring circuits controlled by 32amp MCBs. They are for wall sockets one for each floor.
                      • There will be another 6amp MCB controlling outside light.
                      • Another MCBs you will find there would be of 16amp that controls the heating cylinder or immersion heater if present.
                      • To control shower, you would have an MCB or RCD of 40amp and one for the cooker of minimum 40amp.
                      • The amp used in MCBs or RCDs depends upon what applicants it controls. The heavier the load the higher amp circuit breaker is used to control the normal flow of electricity.
                      Note: In domestic property the consumer unit should not accede to more than `100A`

                      What is the purpose of Testing Earth continuity ?§

                      To verify that the protective bonding in the house is not broken. To measure the continuity of protective conductors a low-resistance ohmmeter is required, typically in the range of `0.2-2 ohms`

                      What is polarity testing ?§

                      To ensure that live is live and neutral is neutral and they do not cross over in circuit. If it happens the circuit will not work.
                      All single pole switches must be in the line conductor and all light Lightings and sockets must be connected the correct way round.

                      • There are many ways to check the polarity of the circuit.
