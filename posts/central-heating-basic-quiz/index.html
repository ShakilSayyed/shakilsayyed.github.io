Central Heating Basic Quiz

November 22, 20227 min read PlumbingQuiz

Contents

Central Heating Knowledge & Quiz§

Level Basic§

Q-1 Bedroom RST should be set to what temperature ?§

20 Degree Centigrade
Bathroom temperature should be set at 23 Degree Centigrade.

Q-2 What amp fuse cartridge is used in fused spar linked to a boiler ?§

The fused spar has 3 amp cartridge
All boilers requires 1 amp. While MCB ( mini circuit board ) is of 6 amp

Q-3 What device reduces possible noise and boiler lock out ?§

Automatic bypass valve
Note: Automatic by pass allows the minimum water flow required by the boiler between flow and return pipe. ABV is used to release the pressure that may have been created due to no other routes present for the water flow, with increase pressure noise is created in the system. If no such route is provided or pressure is not released the boiler will shut down indicating error.

Q-4 What is a W-Plan§

It is a hot water priority motorised valve.
A combination boiler uses a diverter or hot water priority motorised valve.

Q-5 Can we install a TRV on Tower rails in bathroom ?§

No, TRVs are not installed on tower rails.
TRVs are installed in rooms to allow customer to control room temperature individually. It is not installed in a room where RST is fitted.

Q-6 What is a boiler cycling ?§

When the boiler fires up and cools down and carry on doing, it is knowns as boiler cycling.
Boiler cycling puts a lot of stress on the boiler and used to happen in old system where not many controls were present to control the temperature. If it happens in modern boiler it is a sign that boiler is working inefficiently.

Q- 7 What are minibore and microbore system ?§

1. The term Minibore and also small bore is used for R250 copper pipes which comes in sizes of 15, 22 and 28 mm, also known as half hard straight length of copper. In majority of fully pumped system, small bore pipe is used of either 15mm and 22mm.
The term Microbore is used for R220 copper pipe which comes in sizes of 6-8-10 and 12 mm and used with manifolds in some central heating systems where flexibility is required, every radiator has its own loop.

Q-8 Difference between permanent and temporary decommissioning ?§

Temporary decommissioning is needed when a system is worked on for a short period of time. For example replacing a part, draining the system etc.
Permanent decommissioning is needed when system is being taken out of service, dismantled or stripped out.

Q-9 How do you find the grade of Low Carbon Steel ?§

By its color
Grades Color Use
Heavy Red Steam and fire systems
Medium Blue Heating and oil lines
Light Brown Gas

Q-10 Which one of the gas boilers overcomes the need for a cold water storage cistern, f&E cistern and hot water storage vessel ?§

Combination boiler
Cold water is fed from mains, water expansion is accommodated by expansion vessel and pressure release valve and hot water is supplied instantly using plater exchanger heater.

Q-11 When positioning a single thermostat in dwelling what is the recommended height from ground level ?§

1.5m in domestic settings
It must be at least 3 m away from the radiator if present.

Q-12 Why does a filling loop need to have a double check valve included ?§

To protect from contaminating by category 3 water. As it connects category 1 water from category 3 water.
Note: On commercial heating system a Type BA device (PRZ valve) prevention device is used to protect category 1 from category 4 water.

Q-13 In what system the boiler position can be higher than the hot water cylinder ?§

Fully pumped sealed system.

Q-14 Why do you require a filling loop in sealed system ?§

To fill the system with water to a specific pressure.
Note: It usually connects main water feed to a central heating return pipe and is fitted with EC/ED back flow prevention device known as double check valve.

Q-15 The process of adjusting the flow rate though radiators during commissioning is called ?§

Balancing

Q- 16 What is the temperature kept for central-heating and hot water system in modernized or fully pumped systems using Y-Plan ?§

CH is set to 85 degree centigrade while HW is set to 60 degree centigrade
The above specified temperature is set irrespective of the plan and system used.

Q-17 Where are motorised valves in S and Y plans fitted ?§

They are fitted near to the cylinder.

Q-18 Which boiler only controls the heating ?§

A combi boiler
A combi boiler controls the heating only as the hot water is produced on demand instantly when you turn on the tap. There is no need for the cylinder to store the hot water thereby no control over hot water.

Q-19 If a property is over 150 square meter which plan is best suited ?§

S-Plan Plus
It incorporates the needs of an external zone..

Q-20 In sealed system what else is required to accommodate water expansion other than expansion vessel ?§

A PRV will need to be installed.
This allows the system to discharge pressure to a safe location if required.

Q-21 Which two pipes should not contain any valve in vented central-heating system ?§

A cold feed pipe and a vent pipe.
The cold feed pipe coming from the F&E cistern to the boiler should not have any valve to make sure that in case of overheating there is a constant supply of cooler water to the system to prevent the dangerous condition of boiling. Similarly a vent pipe should also not contain any valve at all ( service valve or isolation valve ), and it must rise 450mm above the water level to ensure that the system must remain at atmospheric pressure limiting the temperature to 100 degree centigrade.

Q-22 Which boilers supply hot water indirectly ?§

Traditional and regular boilers
> They supply hot water indirectly via a hot water storage cylinder as compared to combi boiler which supply hot water directly.

Q-24 Under Building Regulation Part L which boilers can be fitted ?§

- Only **SEDBUK** ( Seasonal Efficiency of a Domestic Boiler in UK ) rated A condensing boiler can be fitted.

Q-25 Identify one advantage of underfloor heating§

Even spread of heat.

Q-26 At what pressure is a pressure relief valve on a combination boiler normally set to discharge ?§

3 bar

Q-27 Where should be an air vent be installed in hot water system ?§

At the highest point in system

Q-28 What does part J document covers ?§

Heat producing appliances such as boilers.

Q-29 What is the draw back of radiator without fins ?§

Heat is not convected evenly in the room.
Without fins, heat is not convected evenly in the room and leaves the cold patches. It is these fins that traps the air which is convected in the room. Due to having fins 70 heat is convected.

Q-30 What is church or hospital radiators ?§

Column radiators
Church or Hospital radiator were the name given to column radiators because they were installed in these places, they take lot of water and take longer to warm up.

Q-31 Which radiators are installed in places where vulnerable people are present ?§

Low Surface temperature radiators

Q-32 What document says that radiators installed on vulnerable places should not have surface temperature more than 43 degree centigrade ?§

HSE Health and Safety information sheet no 6.

Q-33 Radiators technical specification , testing and rating is controlled by what standard ?§

BS EN 4421

Q-34 Which is more efficient system sealed or vented ?§

Sealed system.
It is quicker to heat up the dwellings but both are same when it comes to measure energy efficiency.

Q-35 Why a system called open vented ?§

Open vented refers to the separate vent pipe which is open to the atmospheric pressure.
